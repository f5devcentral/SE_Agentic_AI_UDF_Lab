City: Barcelona
Theme: Nature and outdoor activities
Season: Spring

1) Montjuïc Hill and Botanical Gardens
- Description: Montjuïc is a large hill overlooking Barcelona’s harbor with parks, gardens, and panoramic viewpoints. In spring, the botanical gardens are full of Mediterranean species, and the temperatures are mild enough for long walks without heat stress.
- Why it fits a nature lover: You get greenery, views of the sea, and quiet paths away from crowded shopping streets.
- Budget: Access to Montjuïc itself is free; you only pay for cable cars or specific attractions. You can easily spend half a day here with very low cost.
- Tips: Pack water and comfortable walking shoes; sunsets from Montjuïc Castle are especially beautiful.

2) Parc de Collserola Hiking
- Description: Parc de Collserola is a huge natural park behind Barcelona with many hiking trails, forests, and viewpoints. It is directly reachable with commuter trains from the city center.
- Why it fits a nature lover: You can do several short hikes or a full-day loop, with forest shade and birdsong just a few kilometers from the city.
- Budget: Public transport to the park is inexpensive; hiking is free.
- Tips: In spring, mornings and late afternoons are ideal. Bring a light jacket, as temperatures can drop in the evening.

3) Beach Walks and Coastal Paths
- Description: The Barcelona coastline provides a long, continuous promenade with cycling and walking paths. While urban, it still offers sea views, fresh air, and easy access.
- Why it fits a nature lover: You can walk for hours along the waterfront, watch sunrise or sunset over the sea, and combine this with short swims when the weather allows.
- Budget: Free, apart from snacks or drinks.
- Tips: Early mornings in spring are quieter and cooler. Stay hydrated and use sun protection even on cloudy days.

4) Day Trip to Montserrat
- Description: Montserrat is a mountain range with unique rock formations about one hour from Barcelona by train. It has a monastery, hiking trails, and viewpoints over Catalonia.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Rugged mountain scenery, quiet trails, and panoramic views make it ideal for a full-day nature trip.
- Budget: You pay for train and cable car or rack railway, but once at the top, the trails are free. It is an excellent value day trip for budget-conscious travelers.
- Tips: Check weather for rain or fog; some trails can be slippery. Good hiking shoes are recommended.

5) Parc de la Ciutadella and Urban Green Pockets
- Description: Parc de la Ciutadella is a central park with a small lake, palm trees, and shady areas. It is not wilderness, but it is a relaxing green break inside the city.
- Why it fits a nature lover: For days when you do not want to leave the city, you still get trees, water, and birds.
- Budget: Free to enter; optional small boat rentals on the lake.
- Tips: Spring weekends can be busy; mornings are quieter.

Budget and Duration Considerations
- For a one-week spring trip under $1000, combining free or low-cost nature activities like Montjuïc, Collserola, and beach walks helps keep the total cost down while still offering variety.
- A typical pattern could be: two full hiking days (Collserola + Montserrat), two half-days in city parks, and several coastal walks spread across the week.
