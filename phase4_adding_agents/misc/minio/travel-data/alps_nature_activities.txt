Region: Alps (example: Chamonix / Alpine town)
Theme: Mountain and nature activities
Season: Spring (shoulder season)

1) Valley Floor Nature Walks
- Description: Many Alpine towns offer flat or gently sloping walking paths along rivers and valley floors. These are ideal in early spring when higher trails may still have snow.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Rivers, forests, open meadows, and mountain views without technical hiking.
- Budget: Most paths are free; you only pay for local transport within the valley if needed.
- Tips: Check local tourist office maps for marked paths; spring meltwater can make some sections muddy.

2) Low-Elevation Forest Hikes
- Description: Trails through mixed forests at lower elevations, often starting directly from town or nearby villages.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Birdsong, fresh spring foliage, and less risk of snow/ice than on high ridges.
- Budget: Free; consider picnic lunches to save money.
- Tips: Wear waterproof footwear; spring weather can change quickly.

3) Scenic Cable Car + Short Walk
- Description: Take one cable car or gondola to a mid-station, then do a short panoramic walk.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Big views with moderate effort, good if you want a “wow” day without a long climb.
- Budget: Cable cars can be the biggest single daily cost; choose one or two key days rather than every day if you are budget-conscious.
- Tips: Check off-season discounts or half-day tickets.

4) Lakeside Trails (if near a lake)
- Description: Some Alpine areas have nearby lakes with loop paths and picnic spots.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Calm water, reflections of mountains, birdlife.
- Budget: Free; small cost if you rent a bike or a rowing boat.
- Tips: Spring water can be very cold; swimming is for the brave or for summer.

5) Short Nature Interpretation Trails
- Description: Some regions maintain educational nature trails with signs about plants, animals, and geology.
- Why it fits a nature lover: Extra context makes each walk more meaningful and interesting.
- Budget: Usually free.
- Tips: Combine with a picnic to make a low-cost half-day activity.

Budget and Planning Notes
- In Alpine destinations, accommodation and cable cars can eat budget quickly.
- For a one-week trip under 1000 USD, prioritize:
  - Free valley and forest walks.
  - One or two key cable car days.
  - Self-catered meals when possible.
